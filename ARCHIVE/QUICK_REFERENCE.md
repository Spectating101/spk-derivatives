## BODY LANGUAGE

- **Opening:** Stand center, eyes up, slow pace
- **Problem:** Move toward graph, point, slow down, let stakes sink
- **Solution:** Use shield motion with hand (protection gesture)
- **Validation:** Back to clinical tone, let math speak
- **Implications:** Step away from screen, big picture gestures
- **Closing:** Return to center, plant feet, powerful silence

---

## WHAT NOT TO SAY

❌ Advanced options theory
❌ Black-Scholes formula
❌ Stochastic volatility
❌ Greeks (Delta, Gamma, Vega)
❌ Local volatility surfaces

✅ "Same math Wall Street uses"
✅ "Insurance for energy"
✅ "Two methods converge"
✅ "99% error reduction"
✅ "Physics meets finance"

## DECISION TREE: Which Graph to Show First?

```
"I just showed you a 4-graph summary. Let me walk you through each."

TOP-LEFT first? → YES (sets the problem)
  ↓
BOTTOM-LEFT second? → YES (proves math is right)
  ↓
BOTTOM-RIGHT third? → YES (proves method is robust)
  ↓
TOP-RIGHT last? → YES (shows location variation)
```

This order is: Problem → Validation → Validation → Implications.
Most persuasive sequence.
