## SECTION 9: HANDLING SKEPTICAL QUESTIONS

**Duration:** Variable (prep material, not presentation)
**Purpose:** Anticipate objections and provide clear, concise answers

### Q: "Aren't these prices wrong? Real energy costs way more."

**Answer:**
```
"Good question. You're looking at RETAIL grid prices—what your electricity bill shows.
Our model prices WHOLESALE energy at the source, based on NASA satellite data.

The difference? Retail includes:
- Grid infrastructure costs
- Transmission and distribution
- Taxes
- Subsidies
- Regulatory fees

We intentionally price just the PHYSICS—the raw energy production value.
Why? Because that's what should back blockchain. The pure, undistorted anchor.
Not government-affected market prices."
```

### Q: "How accurate is NASA data?"

**Answer:**
```
"NASA satellite data is highly reliable—it's used for climate research globally.
More importantly: we validate it two ways.
Two completely different mathematical methods converge within 1.3%.
If the data was corrupted, the methods wouldn't agree.
The validation IS the accuracy check."
```

### Q: "Why does Brazil cost 9x more than Germany?"

**Answer:**
```
"Volatility. German weather is stable—sun is predictable year-round.
Brazilian weather is chaotic—high tropical variability.
Higher volatility = higher price uncertainty = higher insurance cost.
It's physics. We don't set the prices. The sun does."
```

### Q: "Can this actually be used?"

**Answer:**
```
"Yes, immediately. Energy producers can use this to calculate hedging costs.
Energy traders can use this to price derivatives for trading.
Blockchain protocols can use this as a collateral backing model.
It's not theoretical. It's applicable today."
```

### Q: "Why should I trust this over other models?"

**Answer:**
```
"Don't trust my word—trust the graphs.
Show them convergence: Error drops 99% as precision increases.
Show them method agreement: Two independent approaches overlap across all locations.

The answer is no. Agreement this close proves correctness."
```
