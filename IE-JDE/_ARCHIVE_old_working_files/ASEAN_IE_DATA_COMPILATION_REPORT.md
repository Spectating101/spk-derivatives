## 3. PHILIPPINES - REPUBLIC ACT NO. 12023 (VAT on Digital Services)

### Status: ✓ VERIFIED (Law enacted, projections need validation)

**Data Source**: KPMG Tax NewsFlash (October 3, 2024)  
**Official Source**: Republic Act No. 12023, signed October 2, 2024

### 3.1 Law Framework
- **VAT Rate**: 12%
- **Registration Threshold**: PHP 3 million annual gross sales (~$53,000 USD)
- **Applicability**: Nonresident digital service providers (B2C transactions)
- **Digital Services Scope**: Software licensing, streaming, cloud services, online advertising, digital goods

### 3.2 Implementation Timeline
- **Law Published**: October 3, 2024
- **Effectivity**: 15 days after publication (October 18, 2024)
- **IRR Issuance**: Within 90 days of effectivity
- **VAT Enforcement Commencement**: 120 days from IRR effectivity = **Mid-2025 expected**

### 3.3 Projected Revenue Impact (2024-2028)
- **5-Year Projection**: PHP 83.8 billion (~$1.54 billion USD)
- **Annual Average**: PHP ~16.76 billion/year
- **Revenue Allocation**: 5% designated to support local creative industry

### 3.4 Digital Payment Infrastructure (Enabling Factor)
- **Digital Payment Adoption (2024)**: 57.4% of retail transactions (volume), 59.0% (value)
- **2023 Adoption**: 52.8% (volume), 55.3% (value)
- **Merchant Payments**: 66.4% of digital payment volume (dominant use case)
- **QR Ph Merchant Growth**: 148.7% YoY increase in 2024

**Assessment**: RA 12023 is well-documented in law but projections remain theoretical. Real revenue data will only be available post-implementation (mid-2025 onwards). The strong digital payment infrastructure provides confidence in collectibility.

## 4. THAILAND - VAT ON ELECTRONIC SERVICES (VES)

### Status: ⚠ PARTIALLY AVAILABLE

**Available Data Source**: Thailand Revenue Department, Bank of Thailand  
**Data Gap**: Limited access to complete 2023-2024 figures

### 4.1 VES Framework
- **Effective Date**: September 1, 2021
- **Tax Rate**: 7% VAT
- **Registration Threshold**: THB 1.8 million annual revenue
- **Applicability**: Non-VAT registered customers (B2C transactions)
- **Key Mechanism**: Foreign providers cannot claim input tax credits

### 4.2 Revenue Data (Verified)

| Period | Revenue | Total Service Value | Notes |
|--------|---------|---------------------|-------|
| Oct 2021 - Mar 2022 | THB 4.2 billion | — | 6-month initial collection |
| By July 2022 | THB 5.9 billion | THB 85 billion | Full-year trajectory |

**Annualized Impact**: THB 5.9B suggests annual run rate of ~THB 12 billion if maintained

### 4.3 Platform Transparency Measure (Effective January 1, 2024)
- **Special Account Requirement**: E-commerce platforms >THB 1 billion revenue must report merchant income to Thai Revenue Department
- **Covered Platforms**: Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop, Grab
- **Purpose**: Formalize domestic "invisible economy" of micro-sellers

### 4.4 Digital Economy Contribution to GDP
- **2023 Estimate**: 12-13% of GDP
- **Source**: NESDC Digital Economy Satellite Account (DESA)

### 4.5 Data Gaps Identified
- ❌ Complete 2023 annual VES revenue figure
- ❌ Complete 2024 annual VES revenue figure  
- ❌ Platform income reporting compliance data
- ⚠️ Requires direct contact with Thai Revenue Department for current data

**Assessment**: Thailand data is the least comprehensive. While the VES regime is substantial, accessible public reporting appears limited compared to other countries. Recommendation: Contact Tilleke & Gibbins law firm or Thai Revenue Department directly.
