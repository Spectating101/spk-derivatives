### 1. Tax Competition Theory
**Finding**: Destination-based taxation eliminates race to the bottom
**Evidence**: Rates vary 6%-12%, no convergence over 5 years, no strategic interaction (‚àÇR_i/‚àÇt_j=0)
**Contribution**: Challenges Zodrow-Mieszkowski predictions for mobile bases. Destination-basis changes the game fundamentally.

### 2. Optimal Tax Design
**Finding**: Broad bases dominate high rates 30:1
**Evidence**: Indonesia's 4-stream design ‚Üí +$187M premium (p=0.018*). Base-broadening simulation ‚Üí +39.7% revenue vs. rate harmonization ‚Üí +1.3%.
**Contribution**: First empirical validation of Ramsey-Diamond-Mirrlees principles in digital taxation context.

### 3. Fiscal Capacity in Developing Countries
**Finding**: Middle-income countries can successfully tax digital economy despite limited capacity
**Evidence**: ASEAN collected $1.78B in 2024, systems approaching maturity (S-curve 70-90% of asymptote)
**Contribution**: Demonstrates capacity-appropriate design (destination-basis, self-registration, large platforms focus) enables success.
